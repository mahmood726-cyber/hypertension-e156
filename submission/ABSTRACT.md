## Active Body

In Africa-site hypertension randomized trials registered on ClinicalTrials.gov, how often do studies meet a practical shortlist for cheaper and faster delivery? Sixty-five completed, results-posted hypertension trials formed the Africa benchmark, and eight met pre-specified transfer shortlist criteria on enrollment, sites, countries, and prospective registration. We performed a registry-based descriptive scan, compared Africa-site records with a global benchmark, and treated the shortlist proportion as the primary estimand with Wilson intervals. The shortlist proportion was 0.12, with 95% confidence interval 0.06 to 0.23. Median results-reporting lag was 504.0 days, interquartile range 416.0 to 858.0, while South Africa dominated the Africa-site footprint and trial duration remained long. These findings suggest hypertension is a weaker near-term template for cheap and rapid African RCT delivery than malaria or HIV under current registry patterns outside major urban referral centers. Interpretation is limited by registry proxies rather than budgets, sparse result-linked publications, and concentration in South African infrastructure that may not generalize broadly.
